# Narrative Principles

## Main Character

The main character is the user deciding what they want another person to understand.

Ankahe should never make the model, the interface, or the brand feel like the hero. The product is successful when it quietly helps the user say something once, shape it with care, and choose what becomes visible.

## Core Story

Some parts of us are hard to explain in the moment. Ankahe helps turn those parts into a clear manual: what helps, what does not, what to know, and what should remain private.

The story is not "AI understands you". The story is "you stay in charge of what is written, included, private, and shared."

## Product Promises

- Help users compose a manual, not fill a database.
- Make privacy visible at every meaningful step.
- Keep sharing deliberate.
- Treat omitted information as a valid choice.
- Produce an artifact that feels useful outside the app.

## Narrative Tensions

- Structured, but not rigid.
- Warm, but not sentimental.
- Helpful, but not prescriptive.
- Private by default, shareable by choice.
- Beautiful, but never cluttered.

## What To Center

- The user's words.
- Visibility decisions.
- Clear next actions.
- The manual as a useful outcome.
- Calm confidence around privacy.

## What To Decenter

- Generation as spectacle.
- Metrics about the person.
- Progress pressure.
- Over-explaining features on screen.
- Decorative language where a user needs clarity.

## Narrative Check

Before adding a feature, screen, section, or sentence, ask:

- Does this help the user create or understand their manual?
- Does it make privacy clearer?
- Does it reduce friction without taking control away?
- Would the screen still feel calm if the user was writing something sensitive?
- Can anything be removed without harming the task?
