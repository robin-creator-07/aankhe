# Tone

## Baseline Tone

Ankahe is warm, direct, steady, and low pressure.

The user may be writing about work habits, needs, boundaries, conflict, care, or identity. Tone should make the interface feel safe enough to continue without becoming sentimental or clinical.

## Tone By Moment

### Onboarding

Use quiet confidence. Explain the product in plain language and get the user into the task.

Preferred:

"Create a manual for how you work, communicate, and want to be understood."

Avoid:

"Discover the hidden blueprint of your interpersonal self."

### Writing

Use invitations, not commands. Questions can be specific, but they should not presume what the user feels.

Preferred:

"What helps people communicate well with you?"

Avoid:

"What trauma shaped your communication style?"

### Privacy And Visibility

Be explicit and concrete. Do not rely on tone, icons, or color alone.

Preferred:

"Included answers appear in the shared manual. Private answers stay in this browser."

Avoid:

"Your sensitive data is protected."

### Export And Sharing

Make the consequence clear before the action. Sharing should feel intentional.

Preferred:

"This link includes only answers marked included."

Avoid:

"Share your complete profile."

### Errors

Stay calm. Do not blame the user or dramatize the failure.

Preferred:

"We could not create the PDF. Your answers are still here."

Avoid:

"Something went terribly wrong."

## Intensity Rules

- Do not over-celebrate ordinary progress.
- Do not create urgency around personal disclosure.
- Do not frame incompleteness as failure.
- Do not promise transformation.
- Do not use clinical authority.

## Words That Fit

Manual, answer, include, private, omit, visible, share, export, draft, artifact, browser, link, QR code, copy, choose, keep.

## Words To Avoid

Heal, diagnose, assess, score, unlock, optimize, streak, XP, level, personality type, truth, reveal, fix, hack, therapy, treatment, magic.
